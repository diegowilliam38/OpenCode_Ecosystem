# Curated Quantum Computing Resources

Comprehensive collection of high-quality resources for learning and developing quantum computing applications.

## Learning Resources by Level

### Beginner Level

#### Interactive Learning
- **[Quirk](http://algassert.com/quirk)** - Browser-based drag-and-drop quantum circuit simulator with real-time visualization
- **[Quantum Playground](http://www.quantumplayground.net/)** - 3D quantum state visualization tool for up to 22 qubits
- **[CNOT](https://cnot.io/)** - Step-by-step introduction to quantum computing concepts
- **[Interactive Intro to Quantum Computing](https://davidbkemp.github.io/QuantumComputingArticle/)** - Interactive learning by David Kemp

#### Online Courses
- **[Brilliant.org Quantum Computing](https://brilliant.org/courses/quantum-computing/)** - Problem-based learning curated with Microsoft and Alphabet X
- **[Understanding Quantum Computers](https://www.futurelearn.com/courses/intro-to-quantum-computing)** - Introduction with minimal mathematics
- **[QC101 on Udemy](https://www.udemy.com/qc101-introduction-to-quantum-computing-quantum-physics-for-beginners/)** - Introductory course on quantum cryptography and programming

#### Books
- **[Quantum Computing: A Gentle Introduction](http://mmrc.amss.cas.cn/tlb/201702/W020170224608150244118.pdf)** - Requires only basic college math
- **[Quantum Computing Explained](https://www.amazon.com/Quantum-Computing-Explained-David-McMahon/dp/0470096993)** - Conversational approach with worked solutions

### Intermediate Level

#### MOOCs
- **[Quantum Information Science I](https://www.edx.org/course/quantum-information-science-i)** - Foundational course on quantum information and computation
- **[Quantum Mechanics and Quantum Computation](https://www.edx.org/course/quantum-mechanics-quantum-computation-uc-berkeleyx-cs-191x)** - UC Berkeley course on quantum principles
- **[The Introduction to Quantum Computing](https://www.coursera.org/learn/quantum-computing-algorithms)** - Coursera course on quantum algorithms
- **[Quantum Machine Learning](https://www.edx.org/course/quantum-machine-learning)** - Learn quantum benefits for ML

#### Practical Tutorials
- **[Qiskit Tutorials](https://github.com/Qiskit/qiskit-tutorial)** - Jupyter notebooks demonstrating Qiskit usage
- **[Learn Quantum Computation using Qiskit](http://community.qiskit.org/textbook)** - Open-source textbook with algorithms and hardware examples
- **[Quantum Katas](https://github.com/Microsoft/QuantumKatas/)** - Programming exercises for Q# learning

#### Books
- **[Learn Quantum Computing with Python and Q#](https://www.manning.com/books/learn-quantum-computing-with-python-and-q-sharp)** - Introduces quantum computing using Python and Q#
- **[Quantum Computing for Computer Scientists](https://www.amazon.com/Quantum-Computing-Computer-Scientists-Yanofsky/dp/0521879965)** - Accessible to undergraduate CS students

### Advanced Level

#### Research Papers
- **[Quantum Algorithm Implementations for Beginners](https://arxiv.org/abs/1804.03719)** - Implementation guide for quantum algorithms
- **[Opportunities and Challenges for Quantum Machine Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/1708.09757)** - Near-term quantum ML opportunities
- **[Quantum Machine Learning](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/264825604_Quantum_Machine_Learning_What_Quantum_Computing_Means_to_Data_Mining)** - QML and data mining

#### Comprehensive References
- **[Quantum Computation and Quantum Information](http://mmrc.amss.cas.cn/tlb/201702/W020170224608149940643.pdf)** - Nielsen & Chuang comprehensive textbook
- **[John Preskill's Lecture Notes](http://www.theory.caltech.edu/~preskill/ph219/index.html#lecture)** - Caltech Physics 219 course notes
- **[Quantum Algorithm Zoo](http://math.nist.gov/quantum/zoo/)** - Comprehensive catalog of quantum algorithms

#### Advanced Courses
- **[Quantum Computer Programming](https://cs269q.stanford.edu/syllabus.html)** - Stanford hands-on course without quantum mechanics background
- **[MIT Quantum Information Sciences](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/media-arts-and-sciences/mas-865j-quantum-information-science-spring-2006/lecture-notes/)** - MIT lecture notes on quantum information

## Resource Selection Guide

### For Learning Quantum Basics
1. Start with **Quirk** or **Quantum Playground** (interactive visualization)
2. Follow **Brilliant.org** or **CNOT** (structured learning)
3. Read **"Quantum Computing: A Gentle Introduction"** (foundational book)
4. Complete **Quantum Katas** (hands-on practice)

### For Algorithm Development
1. Read **Quantum Algorithm Zoo** (algorithm catalog)
2. Study **Qiskit Tutorials** (practical examples)
3. Implement with **Qiskit** or **Cirq** (framework choice)
4. Reference **Nielsen & Chuang** (comprehensive theory)

### For Quantum Machine Learning
1. Read **"Quantum Machine Learning for Data Scientists"** (overview)
2. Explore **PennyLane** or **TensorFlow Quantum** (frameworks)
3. Study **"Opportunities and Challenges for QML"** (research)
4. Implement with **quantum_classifier.py** or **quantum_embeddings.py** (practice)

### For Research
1. Read **John Preskill's Lecture Notes** (theoretical foundation)
2. Study **arXiv papers** (latest research)
3. Attend **conferences** (community engagement)
4. Join **research groups** (collaboration)
